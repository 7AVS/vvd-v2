## Key Finding 1: VCN Overcontacting

VCN averages 9.88 contacts per client with returns collapsing after deployment 3.

- Deployment 1: 1.86% success rate
- Deployment 10: 0.33% success rate (-82% decline)
- 61.3% of volume (deployments 3-10) captures only 17.6% of results
- First 2-3 contacts capture 70% of all results

This is the single largest inefficiency in the VVD program. The majority of VCN spend is directed at contacts that produce negligible returns.

---

## Key Finding 2: Volume-Performance Inverse

There is an inverse relationship between campaign volume and campaign performance.

- VCN has 82.9% of total volume but only a 0.60% success rate
- VDT has 1.2% of total volume but a 59.6% success rate
- Reallocation from VCN to VDT-style targeting could yield up to 100x improvement in efficiency

The program is structurally over-indexed on its worst-performing campaign.

---

## Key Finding 3: No Campaign Orchestration

There is no evidence of designed cross-campaign orchestration.

- 41% of clients experience overlapping campaigns (< 25 day gap)
- VCN-to-VCN self-loops dominate with 1.67M transitions
- No designed journey exists -- clients receive repeated VCN deployments without progression
- 1.3M clients (32%) have true campaign overlaps

The absence of orchestration means clients are receiving uncoordinated, repetitive communications.

---

## Key Finding 4: Diminishing Returns

Success rates peak at 3-4 contacts and decline 30% after the 5th contact.

- 24.8% of clients receive 11+ contacts (confirmed over-contacting)
- Optimal frequency: 3-4 contacts with 30-day cooling periods
- Frequency capping at 5 contacts could reduce volume by 40% while maintaining success rates

The data supports a hard cap on contact frequency as an immediate efficiency lever.

---

## Key Finding 5: Demographic Targeting Opportunity

Significant performance variation exists across demographic segments, but current targeting does not exploit it.

- Age 18-24: 5X better response than 65+ across campaigns
- New customers (0-30 days tenure): 6.35% success vs. 10+ years tenure: 0.39%
- Yet 63.9% of VCN volume targets 10+ year customers
- Student segments consistently outperform (VDT Student Email: 76.74% response rate)

The program is sending the most volume to the least responsive demographic segments.

---

## Key Finding 6: Channel Effectiveness

Channel selection has a material impact on campaign performance.

- Email dramatically outperforms desktop in VDT
- VDA Email+Banner: 2.56% vs. Banner-only: 0.71% (3.6X improvement)
- VDT Desktop actually underperforms control in some segments

Channel optimization represents an untapped lever for improving results without changing targeting.

---

## Key Finding 7: VUT Should Be Discontinued

VUT is the only campaign with uniformly negative results.

- Negative lift: -0.2%
- Negative revenue: -$12K
- Negative ROMI: -18X
- Not statistically significant, but the direction is consistently negative

There is no evidence that VUT drives any incremental value. Continuing it generates cost with no return.

---

## Key Finding 8: VAW Shows Promise

VAW is the newest campaign but demonstrates strong early results.

- Absolute lift: +2.86%
- Relative improvement: 92.8%
- Statistical significance: 99.9%
- Strong demographic targeting potential:
  - Age 18-24: 7.02% success rate
  - Income $150K+: 7.10% success rate

VAW should be scaled, particularly among younger and higher-income segments.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate
- Discontinue VUT (negative returns across all metrics)
- Cap VCN at 3 contacts per client (eliminate 61.3% of wasted volume)
- Shift budget allocation toward VDT-style trigger targeting

### Short-term
- Implement 30-day cooling periods between contacts
- Deploy age-based segmentation (prioritize 18-34)
- Focus acquisition campaigns on new customers (0-90 day tenure)

### Medium-term
- Redesign campaign orchestration with proper client journeys
- Eliminate VCN-to-VCN self-loops
- Introduce channel optimization (email-first strategy for VDT/VDA)

### Long-term
- Implement predictive targeting models to replace rule-based segmentation
- Build real-time feedback loops between campaign deployment and success measurement
- Create a unified customer journey engine with cross-campaign awareness
