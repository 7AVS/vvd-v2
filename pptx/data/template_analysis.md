## 1. Overall Deck Structure & Branding

### Branding Elements
- **Logo**: "next best action" logo in bottom-right corner of every content slide (small, grey circular logo)

### Title Slide Pattern
- Campaign name in large dark blue text (approx 36-40pt)
- Mnemonic on second line in smaller dark blue (approx 18pt): "Mnemonic: DMT"
- Presenter name in dark blue, positioned lower-left
- Background: White with decorative curved lines (blue, gold, grey) sweeping across bottom-right quadrant
- Very minimal -- mostly white space

## 5. Chart Styles

### Vintage/Time Series Curves
- **Line charts** with time on X-axis (dates: 11/10/2025, 11/30/2025, 12/31/2025)
- **Test/Action line**: Solid blue, medium weight
- **Control line**: Dashed red, medium weight
- Data point labels at key positions (start, middle, end values as percentages)
- Light grey gridlines, no box border
- Chart background: White or very light grey
- Y-axis: Percentage scale (0.0% to max)
- X-axis: Date labels

### Annotation Style on Charts
- **Star icon** (orange/gold) next to lift value = statistically significant
- **Lift callout**: "0.27% Lift" with bracket between Test and Control endpoints
- **KPI box**: Blue rounded rectangle with white bold text: "Incremental 917 Acct. $827M"
- **Historical reference**: Grey circle callout: "Historical result of TAO Lift -> 0.82%"
- **Population callout**: Grey circle with "Action Pop 118k"
- Labels are OUTSIDE the chart area, connected visually to the data points

### Pie Charts
- Clean flat colors (no 3D, no shadows)
- Segment labels with percentage: "Control 10%", "High-Action 2%"
- Colors: Dark blue, medium blue, light blue, grey for segments
- Legend below chart

### Horizontal Bar Charts (in tables)
- Inline within table cells
- Green fill = above benchmark
- Blue fill = below benchmark
- Values displayed as text next to or within bars

## 6. Content Density & White Space

### Overall Density
- **Moderate to high** content density on results slides (lots of data)
- **Low density** on title slides (mostly white space)
- Despite high data density, slides do NOT feel cramped because of:
  - Generous margins (approx 0.5-0.7 inch on all sides)
  - Clear zone separation (panels divided by lines)
  - Consistent alignment grid
  - White space between chart and annotations

### Margins & Spacing
- Top margin: ~0.4 inch (campaign ID line)
- Left/right margins: ~0.5 inch
- Bottom margin: ~0.4 inch (logo + page number)
- Panel divider: Thin line, not thick border
- Inter-element spacing: ~0.2-0.3 inch between title and content area

### Content Zones (Top to Bottom)
1. **Campaign ID line** (10pt grey, top-left) -- 0.4 inch
2. **Headline zone** (bold title + subtitle + rule) -- ~1.0 inch
3. **Content zone** (charts, tables, visuals) -- ~5.5 inches (bulk of slide)
4. **Footer zone** (logo + page number) -- 0.4 inch

## 7. Narrative Flow & Story Structure

### Per-Slide Story Pattern
Every content slide follows the same narrative formula:
1. **Headline = The Finding** (not a topic label). Always a complete sentence or statement.
   - GOOD: "Propensity Test results highlight an opportunity to boost performance"
   - GOOD: "Over 45 age group: DAO driving >2x higher lift among segment"
   - GOOD: "Action group Attrition rate hovers above Control Group"
   - BAD (never seen): "Campaign Results" or "Test Results"
2. **Subtitle = The So-What or Implication**
   - "Initial attrition advantage for Single Comms neutralize after four months"
   - "Consider expanding population, increase Control group percentage, and relaxing resting rules"
3. **Visual = The Evidence**
   - Charts, tables, or diagrams that PROVE the headline

### Cross-Slide Narrative (Observed Deck Flow)
1. **Title slide** -- Campaign name + mnemonic + presenter
2. **Setup/context slide** -- Problem statement, test design, population description
3. **Overall results** -- Primary KPI with vintage curve, Test vs Control
4. **Segment deep-dive** -- Break results by segment (age, value tier, etc.)
5. **Feature analysis / profiling** -- What characterizes responders (decision trees, feature importance)
6. **Creative / channel test** -- If applicable, which creative or channel performed better
7. **Profiling + Next Steps** -- Responder demographics, observations, numbered action items

### Key Presentation Conventions
- Headlines use **action verbs and findings**, not topic labels
- Every chart has **explicit annotation** of the key numbers (no "read the chart yourself")
- **Significance is always called out** with star icon + confidence level (95%, 99.9%)
- **Incremental impact always monetized** where possible ($827M, $466M, etc.)
- **"No significance" findings are stated plainly** -- not hidden or spun
- Recommendations are **numbered and specific** (not vague)
- Footnotes explain methodology caveats (e.g., "* Inaccurate due to Apple privacy inflating Opens")

## 10. Summary: Template Rules

1. **Every slide has a finding headline**, not a topic label
2. **Blue = Action/Test, Red dashed = Control** -- ALWAYS
3. **Star icon = statistically significant** (with confidence level)
4. **Campaign ID top-left, logo + page number bottom-right** on every content slide
5. **Horizontal rule** separates headline from content
6. **Two-panel layout** is the default for comparison slides
7. **Monetize impact**: Show dollar values in blue KPI callout boxes
8. **Numbered recommendations**: 1, 2, 3 format in recommendation slides
9. **Clean sans-serif font**, no decorative typography
10. **White background**, minimal color -- let the data speak
11. **Annotate charts explicitly** -- call out the key numbers directly on the visual
12. **State negative findings plainly** -- "No statistical significance" is fine, don't spin
